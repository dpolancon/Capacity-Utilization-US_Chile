# Chile–US Copper Relation, 1900–1971

## Framing
This is the main case corridor opened by the current corpus.

It joins together:
- US industrial and refining centrality,
- American-controlled mining capital in South America,
- Chilean state attempts to capture more of copper-derived surplus,
- long conflict over price, taxation, investment, refining location, and political sovereignty.
