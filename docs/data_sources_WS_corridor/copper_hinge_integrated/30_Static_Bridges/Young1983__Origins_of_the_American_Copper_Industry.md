## One-sentence function
This is a **deep prehistory note** on how US copper capacity emerged through geology, smelting bottlenecks, transport constraints, imported ores, and industrial learning.

## Core claim
The early US copper industry was not born as a self-sufficient national powerhouse. It emerged through a difficult historical process shaped by ore types, Swansea smelting dominance, imported technical knowledge, and the gradual overcoming of domestic bottlenecks.

## Why it matters for this project
This note blocks the temptation to begin the Chile/US relation too late. It shows the US side had to be historically built, and that Chilean ores already mattered within that earlier formation process.

## Strongest extractable mechanisms
- geology and ore type matter for industrial trajectory;
- smelting capability is a decisive bottleneck;
- tariff, transport, and coal access shape industrial viability;
- imported Chilean ores matter before later corporate-imperial consolidation.

## Best use in writing
Use as **structural prehistory** for the US side of the relation.

## Limits / warnings
Not directly a Chile-centered text. It should be paired with Pettengill and Mamalakis.
