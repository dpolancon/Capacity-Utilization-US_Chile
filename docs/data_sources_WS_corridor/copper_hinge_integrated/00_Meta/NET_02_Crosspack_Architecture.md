# Crosspack Architecture

## Purpose
This note explains how the networked bundle is structured and how the two packs should articulate.

## Architecture principle
The bundle is built as **two autonomous packs plus a controlled bridge layer**.

### Pack A
[[USMF_00_Hub]]
- explains the U.S. pole as militarized Fordism / Cold War capitalism
- uses military demand, labor-market tightening, inflation politics, and strategic-state logic

### Pack B
[[COP_00_Dynamic_Hub]]
- explains copper as a material corridor in the Chile–US relation
- uses refining/trade centrality, stockpiling, multinational allocation, appropriation conflict, and measurement discipline

## Bridge variables
- military demand
- strategic-material status
- stockpiling / procurement
- refining and trade centrality
- multinational allocation
- appropriation share
- sovereignty pressure
- terms-of-trade / external mediation sensitivity

## Why this architecture matters
It prevents two failures:
1. the U.S. pack becoming a detached Cold War macro-history
2. the copper pack becoming a self-contained sector story without macro-political articulation

## Networked but modular
The two packs should remain internally coherent.
The bundle adds navigation and articulation, not conceptual fusion-by-chaos.
