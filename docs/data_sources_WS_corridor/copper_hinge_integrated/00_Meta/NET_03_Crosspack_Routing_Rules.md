# Crosspack Routing Rules

## Rule 1 — Enter through hubs or ledgers
Preferred entry nodes:
- [[NET_00_Bundle_Hub]]
- [[NET_01_Bundle_Ledger]]
- [[USMF_00_Hub]]
- [[COP_00_Dynamic_Hub]]

## Rule 2 — Cross packs only through official bridge nodes
Official bridge nodes:
- [[USMF_05_Relation_to_Copper_Cluster]]
- [[COP_06_Relation_to_USMF_Pack]]

Avoid spontaneous many-to-many cross-linking unless a new bridge note is explicitly justified.

## Rule 3 — Use scale before synthesis
Before merging an insight across packs, check:
- [[USMF_09_Nested_Temporalities]]
- [[COP_04_Nested_Temporalities]]

Do not conflate conjuncture, cycle, and regime.

## Rule 4 — Use contradiction matrices comparatively, not illustratively
Use:
- [[USMF_10_Dynamic_Contradiction_Matrix]]
- [[COP_05_Dynamic_Contradiction_Matrix]]

to compare pressures across packs.
Do not use one side merely to illustrate the other.

## Rule 5 — Preserve measurement discipline on the copper side
Consult:
- [[Shaikh_Basu_Measurement_Constitution]]
- [[Rivas_Critical_Use]]
- [[Rivas_Qualified_Inclusion]]

before importing rent, stock, or profitability claims into the network interpretation.

## Rule 6 — Preserve anti-natural-rate discipline on the U.S. side
Consult:
- [[USMF_06_Guardrails]]
- [[USMF_03_BakerPollinZahrt1996_Positioning]]

Do not treat “full employment” as a fixed natural benchmark.

## Rule 7 — Methodological reserve
Broader UP political-economy accounts remain nearby but distinct until the methodological core is fully stabilized.
This bundle is not a license to ingest the whole UP archive into the copper corridor.
