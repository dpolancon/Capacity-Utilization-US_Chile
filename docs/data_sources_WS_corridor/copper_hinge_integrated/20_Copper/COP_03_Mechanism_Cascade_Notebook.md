# Copper Relational Cluster — Mechanism Cascade Notebook

## Purpose
This note extracts the recurrent causal sequence of the copper corridor from chronology.
Its job is to explain how copper mediates accumulation, appropriation, strategy, and conflict.

## Core cascade
1. U.S.-centered demand and processing power create a corridor for copper.
2. Strategic-state priorities increase the value of secure access and stockpiling.
3. Multinational control over production/refining/trade shapes allocation and appropriation.
4. Chilean efforts to capture more surplus intensify distributive and political conflict.
5. Multinational response may alter output, investment, or allocation in ways not reducible to local profitability.
6. The corridor is reconfigured through political rupture, but not simply abolished.

## Mechanism blocks

### M1 — Refining and trade centrality
- Definition: copper power is exercised not only at the mine, but through refining, treatment, and commercial intermediation.
- Why it matters: this explains why the U.S. side cannot be reduced to ownership alone.
- Main anchors: [[Pettengill1935__US_Foreign_Trade_in_Copper]]

### M2 — Strategic-state enrollment
- Definition: copper becomes a strategic material within Cold War procurement and stockpile logic.
- Why it matters: this connects sector history to U.S. militarized Fordism without reducing one to the other.
- Main anchors: [[Narayan1967__US_Stockpile_Policy_and_the_International_Copper_Industry]], [[COP_06_Relation_to_USMF_Pack]]

### M3 — Appropriation struggle
- Definition: taxation, exchange, investment, and surplus retention become contested channels through which the host state attempts to capture more from copper.
- Why it matters: this is the core Chile-side developmental mechanism.
- Main anchors: [[Mamalakis1967__American_Copper_Companies_and_the_Chilean_Government]]

### M4 — Multinational allocation and perverse response
- Definition: local political pressure can generate corporate responses that redistribute output or investment strategically across a larger corporate field.
- Why it matters: this blocks naive readings based on local profitability alone.
- Main anchors: [[Hojman1982__Perverse_Supply_Response]]

### M5 — Rent as specific, not exhaustive
- Definition: mining rent is real and important, but it is not the exhaustive principle of all sectoral differential outcomes.
- Why it matters: this is the key anti-Rivas guardrail.
- Main anchors: [[Rivas_Critical_Use]], [[Rivas_Qualified_Inclusion]]

### M6 — Measurement discipline
- Definition: stock, rent, profitability, and appropriation estimates are admissible only if translated into explicit valuation, depreciation, depletion, and sector-boundary rules.
- Why it matters: keeps empirical architecture without ontological drift.
- Main anchors: [[Shaikh_Basu_Measurement_Constitution]]
