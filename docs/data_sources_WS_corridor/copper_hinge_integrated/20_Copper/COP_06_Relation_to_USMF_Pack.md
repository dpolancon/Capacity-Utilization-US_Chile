# Copper Relational Cluster — Relation to US Militarized Fordism Pack

## Purpose
This is the official relay note between the copper cluster and the US militarized Fordism pack.
Its job is to articulate the two packs without allowing either one to swallow the other.

## Two-way articulation

### From USMF to copper
[[USMF_05_Relation_to_Copper_Cluster]] shows how U.S. militarized demand, strategic-state priorities, and defense legitimacy make strategic materials politically salient.
This copper-side note specifies how that salience is materially mediated:
- through refining and trade centrality
- through stockpiling and procurement
- through multinational control and allocation
- through struggles over appropriation and sovereignty in Chile

### From copper to USMF
The copper corridor gives USMF a material corridor rather than a purely macro abstraction.
It shows how a militarized macro regime depends on specific sectors, circuits, and geopolitical-material relations.

## Relay sequence
U.S. militarized demand/state priorities
→ strategic-material logic
→ stockpiling/procurement
→ refining/trade centrality
→ multinational control
→ Chilean appropriation struggle
→ political conflict over accumulation and sovereignty

## Bridge variables
- military demand
- strategic-material status
- refining/trade centrality
- appropriation share
- multinational allocation behavior
- sovereignty pressure
- external-price and terms-of-trade sensitivity

## Use rule
Route all cross-pack interpretation through this note.
Do not connect every USMF note directly to every copper note.

## Guardrail
The copper corridor is not just an illustration of US militarism.
The US militarized Fordism pack is not just background for a Chilean mining story.
The relation works both ways.
