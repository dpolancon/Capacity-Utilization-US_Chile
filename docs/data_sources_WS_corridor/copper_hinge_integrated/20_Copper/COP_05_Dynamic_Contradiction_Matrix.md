# Copper Relational Cluster — Dynamic Contradiction Matrix

## Purpose
This note tracks the contradictions activated, displaced, or intensified across the major phases of the copper corridor.

## Contradiction set

### C1 — Foreign control vs national appropriation
- Definition: tension between multinational control of copper and Chilean attempts to retain more surplus.

### C2 — Market allocation vs strategic-state intervention
- Definition: tension between ordinary market mediation and Cold War strategic-material governance.

### C3 — Refining/trade centrality vs extraction-site sovereignty
- Definition: tension between control over downstream circuits and political control at the extraction site.

### C4 — Local profitability vs multinational allocation logic
- Definition: tension between host-country profitability signals and broader corporate strategy.

### C5 — Rent specificity vs general capitalist differentials
- Definition: tension between the real role of mining rent and attempts to make rent the exhaustive explanatory principle.

### C6 — Measurement utility vs ontological overreach
- Definition: tension between using sectoral estimates productively and turning them into a total explanatory shell.

## Matrix

| Phase | C1 | C2 | C3 | C4 | C5 | C6 | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| U.S. capacity formation | low | low | medium | low | low | low | prehistory of corridor construction |
| U.S. hub centrality / Chilean insertion | high | low | high | medium | low | low | foreign control and downstream dominance consolidate |
| Cold War strategic-material consolidation | high | high | high | medium | low | low | state strategy intensifies corridor significance |
| Developmental appropriation struggle | very high | medium | high | medium | medium | low | host-state claims sharpen conflict |

## Reading guide
- low = present but secondary
- medium = active contradiction
- high = structuring contradiction
- very high = contradiction becomes politically decisive
