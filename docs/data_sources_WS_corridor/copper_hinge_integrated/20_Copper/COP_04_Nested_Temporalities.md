# Copper Relational Cluster — Nested Temporalities

## Purpose
This note differentiates temporal scales on the copper side.
Its job is to prevent the conflation of sector episode, political rupture, and regime transformation.

## Temporal levels

### Level 1 — Conjuncture
- Definition: short-lived shocks, stockpile decisions, price spikes, supply responses, diplomatic or fiscal episodes.
- Typical examples: procurement shifts, sudden taxation disputes, price or investment shocks.
- Use in writing: use for episodes, not for regime claims.

### Level 2 — Sectoral cycle
- Definition: medium-run shifts in output, investment, allocation, and bargaining inside the copper corridor.
- Typical examples: changes in multinational strategy, recurring appropriation disputes, investment slowdowns.
- Use in writing: use for recurring sector dynamics.

### Level 3 — Accumulation/regime articulation
- Definition: the longer-run place of copper in Chilean accumulation, U.S. strategic power, and world-market order.
- Typical examples: Cold War strategic-material enrollment; copper as pillar of Chilean external insertion.
- Use in writing: use when copper is part of a broader regime story.

### Level 4 — Political rupture / transition
- Definition: moments when the corridor is politically reconfigured rather than merely adjusted.
- Typical examples: late-1960s/early-1970s escalation and reconfiguration after 1973.
- Use in writing: reserve for true transition or rupture claims.

## Scale discipline
### What not to do
- Do not treat every fiscal or price episode as a regime crisis.
- Do not collapse the entire copper history into the UP.
- Do not confuse long-run strategic-material importance with every conjunctural market movement.

### What to do
- Identify whether the claim concerns a short-run event, a medium-run sectoral dynamic, a regime articulation, or a transition.
