# US Militarized Fordism — Mechanism Cascade Notebook

## Purpose
This note extracts the recurrent causal sequence of US militarized Fordism from chronology.

Its job is to isolate the **causal engine** of the pack in a reusable form.

## Use rule
This note explains motion in causal terms.
It does **not** govern phase chronology; that belongs to [[USMF_07_Cycle_and_Transition_Map]].

## Core cascade
1. **State / military demand rises**
2. **Aggregate demand strengthens**
3. **Labor-market slack narrows**
4. **Workers’ bargaining position improves**
5. **Distribution shifts and/or profitability pressure emerges**
6. **Inflation discourse intensifies**
7. **Elite backlash and restraint pressures grow**
8. **Retrenchment or slowdown follows**
9. **Renewed militarized stimulus regains political viability**

## Mechanism blocks

### M1 — Militarized demand as macro stimulus
- Large defense outlays increase effective demand
- This matters especially when productive capacity and labor are underutilized
- Military demand is politically easier to legitimate than large-scale civilian planning

### M2 — Labor-market tightening
- The relevant object is not a fixed “full-employment rate”
- The relevant object is historically contingent **tight labor-market conditions**
- These conditions improve hiring, wages, bargaining power, and absorption of marginalized workers

### M3 — Distributional conflict
- Tight labor markets alter the balance of class forces
- The resulting conflict is often narrated as “inflation” but is also about distribution, discipline, and profitability
- Wage share and labor power become politically visible problems for elites

### M4 — Retrenchment logic
- When labor gains and inflation fears intensify, restraint becomes politically attractive
- Anti-inflation policy often works as a rollback of labor-market tightness
- Retrenchment restores slack and management leverage

### M5 — Return of militarized demand
- Military expenditure remains unusually admissible as state activism
- It supports demand without directly socializing investment in the stronger civilian sense
- This gives it recurrent political utility across phases

## Controlled terminology

### Preferred terms
- tight labor-market conditions
- high-pressure demand conditions
- labor-market absorption
- distributional conflict
- labor-market tightening

### Suppressed terms
- natural rate of unemployment
- fixed full-employment benchmark
- technically well-defined full employment
