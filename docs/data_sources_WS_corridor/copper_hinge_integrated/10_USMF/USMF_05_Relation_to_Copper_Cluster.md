# US Militarized Fordism — Relation to Copper Cluster

## Purpose
This is the official articulation node between the US Militarized Fordism pack and the copper relational cluster.

Its job is to convert the US-side macro-political architecture into a controlled set of relational questions about copper, strategic materials, refining, stockpiling, multinational organization, and Chilean appropriation struggles.

## Governing principle
US militarized Fordism matters here because it clarifies the **demand/state architecture** into which copper, stockpiling, refining, and Chilean appropriation struggles were inserted.

The copper cluster matters because it gives the **material corridor** through which those US dynamics were mediated.

## Core relay proposition
The bridge does not run from “the United States” in the abstract to “Chile” in the abstract.

It runs through a historically specific relay:

**militarized demand / strategic-state priorities**
→ **strategic-materials governance**
→ **refining, trade, and stockpiling circuits**
→ **multinational control and appropriation struggles**
→ **Chilean state, accumulation, and conflict**

## Why copper is central here
Copper is not admitted merely because it is an important Chilean export.

It is central because it links:
- US defense demand and strategic preparedness
- US refining and market-centrality
- American-controlled mining capital in Chile
- Chilean struggles over taxation, rent, exchange, investment, and sovereignty

That is why this note is the only official bridge from USMF outward.

## What USMF contributes to the copper cluster
- explains why strategic materials mattered on the US side
- specifies the macro-political role of defense demand and strategic procurement
- clarifies how stockpiling can be read as part of a wider Cold War capitalist architecture
- gives a state-demand context for copper beyond firm-level or national narratives
- supplies a dynamic grammar of boom, retrenchment, and transition that can be tested against copper-sector evidence

## What the copper cluster contributes to USMF
- materializes militarized demand in concrete commodity and refining systems
- prevents “military spending” from remaining too abstract
- connects US macro-political architecture to Chilean state/company/conflict dynamics
- gives a concrete corridor for the Chile–US relation
- shows how strategic-state priorities are mediated through ownership, trade, refining, and appropriation

## Operational bridge variables
These are the variables or relational objects the bridge should track.

| Bridge variable | USMF side | Copper side | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| Strategic demand | defense budgets, rearmament, procurement | copper as strategic input | links militarized demand to raw-material relevance |
| Strategic-state intervention | stockpiling, procurement policy, national-security planning | stockpile policy, procurement channels | shows how the state mediates raw-material circuits |
| Processing centrality | industrial/defense demand | US refining, treatment, re-export circuits | prevents raw materials from being treated as mere extraction |
| Ownership/control | military-industrial mixed economy, corporate internationalism | American-controlled copper companies | links state priorities to multinational organization |
| Appropriation conflict | macro demand and geopolitical strategy | taxation, rent, exchange, investment disputes | translates world-political demand into Chilean conflict |
| Regime transition | booms, retrenchments, neoliberal mutation | shifting terms of appropriation and strategic relevance | lets copper be read dynamically rather than statically |

## Phase-sensitive articulation
The bridge should be read dynamically, not as one frozen relation.

### 1. Rearmament / militarized expansion
Question: how does rising defense demand elevate the importance of copper and strategic-material governance?

### 2. Managed retrenchment / slowdown
Question: what happens to strategic-material intensity and procurement priorities when defense budgets are cut or stabilized?

### 3. Conflictual expansion
Question: when militarized demand intensifies again, how are stockpiling, refining, and multinational strategies reactivated or transformed?

### 4. Crisis / transition
Question: when inflation politics, profitability pressure, and regime instability emerge, does copper remain central, shift function, or become rearticulated through new channels?

## Nested-temporality bridge rule
Before using any copper evidence through the USMF bridge, identify the temporal scale:
- conjuncture
- political-economic cycle
- regime
- transition

Use [[USMF_09_Nested_Temporalities]] before making outward claims.

## Admissible evidence lanes
### Lane A — strategic-state evidence
Use for stockpiling, procurement, defense preparedness, strategic-material designation.

### Lane B — processing and trade evidence
Use for refining, treatment, re-export, US market-centrality.

### Lane C — ownership and investment evidence
Use for multinational control, American company strategy, location of capacity.

### Lane D — appropriation conflict evidence
Use for taxation, rent, exchange, investment, nationalization pressures.

### Lane E — dynamic conflict evidence
Use for output behavior under host-state conflict and multinational strategic allocation.

## Questions this bridge should answer
- Why did copper matter to the US side beyond ordinary trade?
- Through which institutions did military and strategic priorities translate into copper policy?
- How did refining and processing centrality shape the relation rather than simply extraction?
- How were Chilean struggles over appropriation connected to US strategic and macro-political priorities?
- At which moments was copper most tightly articulated to militarized Fordism, and when did the relation mutate?

## Use rule
All outward articulation from the USMF dynamic notes should pass through this note.

Do not create dense many-to-many crosslinks from every USMF note into the copper corpus.

## What this note is not
- not a replacement for the copper hub
- not a summary of all copper notes
- not a license to broaden the cluster to the whole political economy of the UP
- not a general theory of dependency

## Defer / keep at distance
The broader political economy of the rise and fall of the Unidad Popular remains a nearby but distinct reserve cluster.
It may be cross-linked, but should not be actively absorbed here until the methodological core is stabilized.

## Guardrail
This note keeps the relation controlled.
US macro history is admitted here only insofar as it clarifies the Chile–US material articulation.

Copper evidence is admitted here only insofar as it clarifies the strategic-material and appropriation relay.
