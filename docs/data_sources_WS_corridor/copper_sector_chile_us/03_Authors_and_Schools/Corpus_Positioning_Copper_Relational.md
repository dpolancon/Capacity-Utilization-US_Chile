# Corpus Positioning — Copper Relational Cluster

## Functional roles in the current corpus

### Young (1983)
Provides deep prehistory of US copper formation: geology, smelting bottlenecks, transport, imported ores, and the transition toward larger industrial copper capacity.

### Pettengill (1935)
Shows the US becoming a trade, refining, and export hub and highlights the significance of American-controlled mines in South America.

### Mamalakis (1967)
Anchors the Chile-side relational field: state, copper companies, returned revenue, export-sector structure, and development effects.

### Narayan / Reddy (1967)
Adds the US strategic-state layer: stockpiling, strategic materials policy, and world-market effects.

### Hojman (1982)
Provides a compact econometric mechanism note that disrupts standard supply theory.

### Rivas (2022/2023)
Useful for long-run measurement architecture and series assembly, but theoretically overcommitted for present purposes.
