## One-sentence function
This is the key note on the US becoming not just a producer but a **trade, refining, and export hub** within wider copper circuits.

## Core claim
US copper history is not a simple output story. The US became central by combining domestic production with refining, processing, imports for treatment, re-exports, and stronger links to American-controlled South American mines.

## Why it matters for this project
This note lets you conceptualize US power in copper as **market centrality** and **processing intermediation**, not just mine ownership.

## Strongest extractable mechanisms
- imports for refining can reinforce rather than weaken hub power;
- American-controlled mines in South America alter the meaning of foreign production;
- refining location changes who captures commercial and strategic leverage.

## Best use in writing
Use when you need to move from a mining narrative to a **circuit narrative**.

## Limits / warnings
Very descriptive unless actively linked to Chilean political economy.
