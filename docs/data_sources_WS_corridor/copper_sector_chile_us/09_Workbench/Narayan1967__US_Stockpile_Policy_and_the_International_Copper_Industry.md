## Core claim
Copper markets were shaped not only by firms and prices but also by the US national-security state through stockpile policy, procurement, and strategic-materials administration.

## Why it matters for this project
This note prevents the corpus from collapsing into a firm-only or market-only story.

- stockpiling as demand-side world-market pressure;
- direct link between raw-materials policy and foreign policy.
