## One-sentence function
This is the **Chile-side anchor text** for the relational field linking copper companies, the state, foreign exchange, taxation, investment, and development.

## Core claim
Copper matters in Chile not as a simple export commodity but as a structured relation between foreign firms, the Chilean state, external earnings, domestic appropriation, and long-run development effects.

## Why it matters for this project
This note gives the strongest current language for moving from “copper exports” to a **state–capital–development relation**.

## Strongest extractable mechanisms
- returned revenue vs gross export contribution;
- foreign exchange as a mediated development channel;
- changing state treatment of the companies across periods;
- different effects of copper through availability, profitability, and factor-employment channels.

## Best use in writing
Use as the main empirical-historical anchor when discussing Chilean state mediation of the export sector.

## Limits / warnings
Needs pairing with US-side texts to avoid becoming one-sidedly national.
