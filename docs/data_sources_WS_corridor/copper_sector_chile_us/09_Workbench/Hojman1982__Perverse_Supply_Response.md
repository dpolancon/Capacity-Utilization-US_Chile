## One-sentence function
This is a short but powerful **mechanism note** showing that local mining output may respond negatively to local profit-rate incentives under multinational allocation and prolonged host-state conflict.

## Core claim
The conventional positive supply relation can reverse when multinational firms allocate production strategically across subsidiaries while in long conflict with the host government.

## Why it matters for this project
This note gives the corpus a compact analytical wedge against naive marginalist supply reasoning.

## Strongest extractable mechanisms
- local output is not equivalent to local optimization;
- multinational internal allocation matters;
- political conflict can alter supply behavior in systematic ways;
- better lag structure and richer specification shrink the credibility of price-only elasticity stories.
