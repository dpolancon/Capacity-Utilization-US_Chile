# Copper Relational Pack — Home

This pack organizes a **Chile/US copper corpus** around one problem: how to reconstruct copper not as a national commodity story, but as a historically changing **relational field** joining mines, refining, trade, stockpiling, taxation, state policy, multinational strategy, and world-market positioning.

## Strategic use
This vault fragment is built to help with four things.

First, isolate what each text is *actually for*. Some are historical architecture texts, some are state-policy texts, some are mechanism notes, and some are measurement assets.

Second, avoid theoretical drift. The pack deliberately separates **empirical usefulness** from **philosophical overreach**.

Third, convert readings into writing inputs. Each workbench note is already formatted so it can later feed dissertation drafting.

Fourth, keep open a clear methodological distinction: **Rivas may be mined; he does not govern the ontology. Shaikh/Basu governs the measurement audit layer.**

## Current corpus status
The pack currently centers on the following reading cluster:
- Mamalakis on the Chilean state and the export sector
- Narayan/Reddy on US stockpile policy and the international copper industry
- Pettengill on US trade, refining, and American-controlled South American production
- Young on the deep formation of the US copper industry
- Hojman on perverse supply response under multinational conflict
- Rivas as a data-rich but theoretically overcommitted dissertation
- Shaikh/Basu as the measurement guardrail
