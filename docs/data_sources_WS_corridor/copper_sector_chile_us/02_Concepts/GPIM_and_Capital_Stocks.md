# GPIM and Capital Stocks

This note states the methodological baseline for capital-stock handling in this pack.

## Governing source
Use [[ShaikhBasu__GPIM_Method_Note]] as the formal baseline.

## Core distinctions
Always distinguish:
- gross vs net stock,
- historical-cost vs constant-dollar vs replacement-cost stock,
- depreciation vs depletion,
- end-of-period vs mid-year timing.

## Why this matters
Many long-run profit-rate and rent calculations are not directly about “the economy.” They are about the interaction between:
- an output or surplus flow,
- a chosen stock denominator,
- a chosen valuation convention,
- a chosen depreciation regime.

Changing any of those can move the result a lot.

## Practical doctrine
No result enters the writing pipeline until the stock concept has been named explicitly.
