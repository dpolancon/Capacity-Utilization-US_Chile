# Reading Sequence — Copper Relational

## Sequence logic

### 1. Young (1983)
Start here for deep prehistory. This keeps the later US/Chile relation from appearing out of nowhere.

### 2. Pettengill (1935)
Move next to the transition from domestic formation to trade/refining centrality and South American control.

### 3. Mamalakis (1967)
Then shift to the Chile-side export sector and the state-company relation.

### 4. Narayan / Reddy (1967)
Add the strategic-state layer from the US side.

### 5. Hojman (1982)
Read as a compact mechanism note that sharpens the multinational/host-state conflict problem.

### 6. Rivas (critical use only)
Mine for empirical architecture, not governing theory.

### 7. Shaikh/Basu
Read alongside or immediately before extracting any stock-based quantitative procedure from Rivas.
