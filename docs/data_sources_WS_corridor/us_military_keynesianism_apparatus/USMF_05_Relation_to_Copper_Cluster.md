# US Militarized Fordism — Relation to Copper Cluster

## Purpose
This is the official articulation node between the US Militarized Fordism pack and the copper relational cluster.

Its job is to preserve the relational function of the US-side material and stop the pack from drifting into standalone US macro-history.

## Core principle
US militarized Fordism matters here because it clarifies the **demand/state architecture** into which copper, stockpiling, refining, and Chilean appropriation struggles were inserted.

The copper cluster matters because it gives the **material corridor** through which those US dynamics were mediated.

## What USMF contributes to the copper cluster
- explains why strategic materials mattered on the US side
- specifies the macro-political role of defense demand and strategic procurement
- clarifies how stockpiling can be read as part of a wider Cold War capitalist architecture
- gives a state-demand context for copper beyond firm-level or national narratives

## What the copper cluster contributes to USMF
- materializes militarized demand in concrete commodity and refining systems
- prevents “military spending” from remaining too abstract
- connects US macro-political architecture to Chilean state/company/conflict dynamics
- gives a concrete corridor for the Chile–US relation
