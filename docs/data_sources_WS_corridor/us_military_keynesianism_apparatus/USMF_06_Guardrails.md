# US Militarized Fordism — Guardrails

## Purpose
This note records the non-negotiable interpretation rules of the pack.

Its job is to preserve theoretical consistency and prevent source-driven drift.

## Conceptual guardrails

### G1 — No natural-rate slippage
Do not treat “full employment” as a fixed benchmark, natural rate, or technically closed labor-market condition.

Authorized reinterpretation:
- labor-market tightening
- high-pressure demand conditions
- reduction in slack with politically consequential bargaining effects

### G2 — No US recentring
The pack exists to deepen the US pole **for relational purposes**.
It is not a standalone history of the United States.

### G3 — No copper dissolution
The copper cluster remains the material corridor.
The USMF pack clarifies the US-side architecture into which that corridor plugs.

### G4 — No premature methodological expansion
Broader UP political-economy accounts remain nearby but deferred until the methodological core is stabilized.

### G5 — No inflation-as-pure-technique reading
Inflation discourse should be read partly as a distributional and political language, not only as a technical macroeconomic problem.

## Dynamic-layer guardrails
- Do not conflate conjuncture with regime
- Do not turn every boom into a regime
- Do not turn every recession into regime crisis
- Route all external articulation through [[USMF_05_Relation_to_Copper_Cluster]]

## Terminology control
### Preferred terms
- militarized demand
- labor-market tightening
- high-pressure demand conditions
- distributional conflict
- strategic-material corridor
- retrenchment

### Terms to suppress or qualify
- natural rate of unemployment
- fixed full-employment benchmark
- pure postwar prosperity consensus
- inflation as the sole explanatory problem

## Source-specific rules
### Barker 2022
Use as long-run phase architecture and macro-political deepener.
Do not let it swallow the relational function of the pack.

### Baker–Pollin–Zahrt 1996
Use as a Vietnam-era mechanism note.
Translate “full employment” into the project's authorized vocabulary unless quoting directly.
