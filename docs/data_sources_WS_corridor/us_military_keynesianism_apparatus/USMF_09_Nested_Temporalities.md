# US Militarized Fordism — Nested Temporalities

## Purpose
This note differentiates temporal scales inside the pack.

Its job is to prevent the conflation of:
- conjuncture
- cycle
- regime
- transition

## Use rule
Every empirical claim used by this pack should be assignable to one of the temporal levels below.

## Temporal levels

### Level 1 — Conjuncture
- **Definition:** short-run episode of acceleration, slowdown, or stress
- **Typical duration:** months to a few years
- **Typical objects:** demand shock, labor-market tightening, recession, inflation burst
- **Example candidates:** Korean War boom, Vietnam boom

### Level 2 — Political-economic cycle
- **Definition:** medium-run sequence of buildup, conflict, restraint, and recovery
- **Typical duration:** several years to roughly a decade
- **Typical objects:** defense expansion followed by backlash and retrenchment
- **Example candidates:** New Look cycle, late-1960s to 1970s deterioration

### Level 3 — Regime
- **Definition:** longer historical organization of accumulation, state form, and legitimacy
- **Typical duration:** decades
- **Typical objects:** Cold War capitalism / militarized Fordism
- **Example candidates:** high Cold War order

### Level 4 — Transition between regimes
- **Definition:** historical mutation in which the old regime loses coherence and a new organization emerges
- **Typical duration:** uneven, often spanning several conjunctures and cycles
- **Typical objects:** neoliberal transition, post-Cold War mutation
- **Example candidates:** 1970s through Reagan rearmament

## Scale discipline

### What not to do
- do not call every boom a regime
- do not treat every recession as a regime crisis
- do not conflate Vietnam with Cold War capitalism as a whole

### What to do
- specify whether the claim concerns a short-run demand episode, a medium-run policy cycle, a regime form, or a regime transition
