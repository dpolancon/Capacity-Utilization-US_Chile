# Forbidden Theoretical Shortcuts

Do not:

- call observed output productive capacity;
- treat a survey utilization measure as the defining latent object;
- add demand components directly to the capacity law without revising the theory;
- interpret a negative interaction coefficient as proof of concavity;
- equate official operating surplus with Shaikh-adjusted surplus;
- assume every fixed asset builds productive capacity;
- treat the U.S. and Chile mechanisms as identical.
