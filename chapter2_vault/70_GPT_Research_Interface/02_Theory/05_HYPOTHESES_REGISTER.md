# Hypotheses Register

## H1

The capacity effect of capital accumulation is not constant across historical periods.

## H2

Distribution changes the composition or technique embodied in accumulation and therefore changes \(\theta_t\).

## H3

The U.S. post-Fordist period exhibits a different transformation relation from the Golden Age.

## H4

Chile’s capacity formation is constrained by external financing and imported means of production.

## H5

Profitability changes can be decomposed into utilization, capacity productivity, and distribution, but the historical weights differ by regime.

## Falsification conditions

- No stable long-run relation under admissible integration treatment.
- No meaningful variation in \(\theta_t\).
- Reconstruction dominated by anchor choice.
- Boundary-incoherent results.
