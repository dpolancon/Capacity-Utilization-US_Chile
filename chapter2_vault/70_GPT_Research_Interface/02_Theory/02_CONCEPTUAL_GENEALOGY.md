# Conceptual Genealogy

## Marxian reproduction

Provides the distinction between accumulated means of production, realized output, surplus, and the historical organization of accumulation.

## Shaikh

Provides a capital-centered benchmark for reconstructing capacity and utilization and a critical national-account perspective.

## Okishio

Provides a mechanism linking technique choice, mechanization, competition, and disproportional accumulation.

## Vidal and the internally divided firm

Supports the idea that technical and organizational choices are not reducible to a representative optimizing unit.

## Sraffian and supermultiplier debates

Clarify the role of autonomous demand in realized output and accumulation while preserving the distinction between demand and the direct creation of productive capacity.

## Center–periphery and external-constraint traditions

Provide the Chilean architecture in which imported capital goods, foreign exchange, reserves, leverage, and balance-of-payments conditions shape mechanization.
