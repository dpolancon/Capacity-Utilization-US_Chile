### §2.2.5 Synthesis: A Multi-Level Theory of the Crisis Trigger

The four preceding strands do not constitute separate theories of crisis. They constitute a single vertical architecture — four levels of the same contradiction, each specifying the mechanism through which the stagnation tendency operates at a distinct organizational scale.

| Level | Mechanism | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Mode of production | Crisis is endemic; demand stimulus displaces, not resolves | Shaikh (1978); Itoh (1978) |
| Meso/spatial | Spatio-temporal fixes defer crisis across space and time; their closure forces the contradiction back into the domestic accumulation path | Arrighi (1978); Harvey (2001, 2018) |
| Aggregate/macro | The capacity utilization path is the crisis trigger; cumulative disequilibrium transmits through $r_t \equiv \mu_t \cdot b_t \cdot \pi_t$ | Okishio (1961); Weisskopf (1979) |
| Firm/organizational | Multi-scalar contradictions within the firm rule out a stable desired $\mu_t$; the behavioral response is regime-specific | Vidal (2014, 2019) |

The Fordist accumulation regime $\Lambda$ is the institutional settlement that operated across all four levels simultaneously — holding class struggle within distributable bounds, stabilizing the spatial fix through Bretton Woods and dollar hegemony, suppressing the within-firm contradiction through regulated wage relations and monopoly pricing conventions, and keeping $\mu_t$ near enough to normal capacity that stagnation tendencies offset rather than reinforced one another. The question the chapter poses empirically is what happens when that settlement breaks.

The stagnation typology sharpens the question. Basu (2019), redefined in Vidal's terms, identifies four channels through which the tendency registers:

|                   | Excess of Surplus Value       | Deficit of Surplus Value     |
|---|---|---|
| **Demand side**   | Under-consumption/realization | Profit squeeze               |
| **Financial side**| Financial fragility           | Falling normal profit rate   |

Each cell corresponds to a distinct configuration in the Weisskopf decomposition. Under-consumption operates through falling $\mu_t$ with stable $\pi_t$; profit squeeze through rising $\mu_t$ that compresses $\pi_t$; financial fragility through leveraged positions that amplify the $\mu_t$ cycle; falling normal profitability through secular erosion of $b_t$. This grid is the interpretive template for Stage B — which channel dominates per sub-period — and Stage C — which channel drives recapitalization decisions.

The synthesis is therefore precise: capacity utilization is not a cyclical variable to be detrended away. It is the medium through which structural crisis registers at the aggregate level, and its measurement must respect the three channels — demand ($\mu_t$), technology ($b_t$), distribution ($\pi_t$) — through which it operates. A measurement procedure that imposes $\theta = 1$ by construction collapses the demand and technology channels into a single residual, rendering the four-level architecture empirically inoperative. The next section constructs the analytical framework that makes it operational.
