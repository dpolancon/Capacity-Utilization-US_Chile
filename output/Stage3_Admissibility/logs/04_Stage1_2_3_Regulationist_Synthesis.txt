I. Stage 1 — Confinement Architecture (Engine 3.0)

Stage 1 established the structural backbone of the system. Across all
windows, specifications, polynomial bases (raw and orthogonal), and
deterministic variants, the ChaoGrid lattice selected (p = 1, r = 1) as
the global PIC minimum.

This result is robust and invariant. The confinement dimension remains r
= 1 in the full sample, Fordism, and Post-Fordism.

The regulationist implication is decisive: regime transformation does
not operate through a change in the dimensionality of the long-run
manifold. The structural accumulation relation remains singular and
stable in rank.

What changes across regimes is not the existence of the long-run
constraint, but the conditions under which it is dynamically realized.

Stage 1 therefore locks the structural manifold.

II. Stage 2 — Feasibility Geometry (Engine 3.1)

Stage 2 mapped the admissible lattice within the invariant r = 1
structure. It shifted the analysis from identification to geometry.

The full sample exhibits a broad admissible domain. Fordism shows a
compressed but coherent admissible region. Post-Fordism exhibits
pronounced feasibility erosion.

Comparable-domain filtering did not drive these differences.
Orthogonalization did not alter lattice feasibility. The regime
variation arises from computational feasibility and stability margins,
not imposed trimming.

The regulationist interpretation is clear: institutional configurations
reshape the range of dynamically admissible specifications without
redefining the underlying structural relation.

Fordism appears as a stable compression of dynamics. Post-Fordism
appears as a regime of narrowed stability margins.

Stage 2 translates regime theory into empirical geometry.

III. Stage 3 — Dynamic Admissibility (Engine 3.2)

Stage 3 introduced economic admissibility via a double gate:

Gate 1: Combined cointegration tests (Bayer–Hanck). Gate 2: ARDL bounds
tests (error-correction admissibility).

The analysis was conducted under a structural prior: always compare the
full sample to the regime partition (Fordism vs Post-Fordism).

Full Sample

Only Q3 specifications under a constant deterministic component pass the
double gate. Cointegration exists at higher nonlinearity, and
error-correction is admissible. The structural relation is statistically
coherent at the aggregate level.

Fordism (1925–1973)

Multiple Q2 and Q3 specifications pass both gates. Combined tests
strongly reject no cointegration, and ARDL bounds tests reject the I(1)
null robustly. The long-run relation is dynamically operative.

Fordism therefore appears as a regime in which the invariant structural
manifold is stably realized through coherent propagation and adjustment
mechanisms.

Post-Fordism (1974–2023)

Baseline ARDL(1) specifications largely fail. However, auto_ardl
selection with BIC reveals admissible cointegration for constant
specifications once richer lag structures are allowed.

This indicates not the absence of cointegration, but increased dynamic
volatility and dispersed propagation.

Post-Fordism thus appears as a regime where the structural relation
persists, but dynamic realization becomes fragile, potentially
reflecting heteroskedastic or unstable adjustment environments.

IV. Integrated Regulationist Interpretation

Across Stages 1–3, the empirical architecture reveals:

1.  A single structural accumulation manifold (r = 1 invariant).
2.  Regime-dependent admissible domain width.
3.  Regime-dependent stability of error correction.

Regime transformation operates through stability margins and dynamic
propagation, not through changes in structural dimensionality.

Fordism stabilizes error-correction realization. Post-Fordism
destabilizes dynamic propagation without altering the structural rank.

The ChaoGrid engine therefore operationalizes regulationist theory
econometrically: modes of regulation reshape admissible dynamics under
invariant structural confinement.

V. Transition to Inference

Stage 3 suggests that Post-Fordism may exhibit conditional
heteroskedasticity or volatility requiring richer lag structures. The
next stage of inference will therefore address:

-   HAC robust estimation,
-   Conditional heteroskedasticity diagnostics,
-   Bootstrap-based robustness where appropriate.

These refinements do not alter the structural conclusions of Stages 1–3.
They deepen the measurement of stability margins within the invariant
accumulation manifold.

Stage 3 is thus closed at the admissibility level.
